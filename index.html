About the Assignment

This is my second homework assignment for CS 360: Data Visualization during spring 2020 with Sophie Engle. We were given this dataset to create a visualization based off of, ideally one that supports the study's findings. To earn an 'A' grade, we were asked to develop either a parallel coordinates visualization or a scatterplot matrix. Dataset Info

This dataset is the second of several reporting data collected for use in the National Bureau of Economic Research's 2017 study "Mobility Report Cards: The Role of Colleges in Intergenerational Mobility."

I accessed the dataset on February 17th 2020 from the Opportunity Insights website here.

Baseline Cross-Sectional Estimates of Child and Parent Income Distributions by College

This table reports the study's baseline estimates of parents’ and children’s income distributions by college. The study's authors calculate college-level values as means over students in the 1980, 1981 and 1982 birth cohorts. When data for a college from any of these cohorts are incomplete, the study's authors use data from 1983 and 1984 cohorts to obtain an estimate.


Measurement Methods

In this dataset, parental income is measured as the average annual household total income before taxes and transfers (defined as Adjusted Gross Income) and adjusted for inflation to 2015 dollars over the five years when the child turned 15-19. It defines child income as individual labor earnings (sum of wage and self- employment earnings) in 2014. The dataset definen the college each child attends as the institution the child attended most frequently during four calendar years in which the child turned 19-22.

Data Wrangling

After downloading the dataset from Opportunity Insights's website, I used Javascript for some minor data wrangling. I split each college (or row in the original dataset) into five separate rows, one per parent quintile, and filtered out columns I didn't need.
